How to Use ABK’s PubMed Search String Generator

ABK’s PubMed Search String Generator is a powerful tool designed to help you check search terms and generate an advanced search string for PubMed. It processes your Population, Intervention, Comparator(s), and Outcome(s) terms by searching the MeSH database and combining the entry terms with appropriate Boolean operators. This tool is essential for conducting systematic reviews, meta-analyses, or any other research requiring a targeted literature review.

Step 1: Enter Search Keywords

Start by entering your search keywords, including the population or disease under study, intervention, and comparator. You can also input the outcomes you're interested in analyzing. This process is akin to checking search terms in PubMed's advanced search.

Step 2: Click Generate

After entering your terms, click the 'Generate' button. The generator will perform a search word analysis, combing through the MeSH database to create a custom PubMed query. Please be patient as the generator processes your input.

Step 3: Copy to Clipboard

Once the PubMed search string is generated, you can copy it to your clipboard by clicking 'Copy to Clipboard'. This feature makes it easy to transfer your search terms to PubMed.

Step 4: Paste the Term in PubMed

Navigate to PubMed and paste your generated query into the search box. After you enter the search keyword, click ‘Search’ to view the results. You can further refine your search using PubMed's filter options.

Step 5: Analyze and Cite

Review the search results tailored to your research question. Utilize the 'Cite My Work' feature to cite the articles in APA format or any other required citation style. If you need to build a reference list, consider using a free citation builder or APA builder.

Additional Tips:

  • Check Search History: Review your PubMed search history to refine your search results further.
  • Find a Date: Use the find a date or website date options for accurate publication referencing.
  • Help and Support: For more assistance, consult the help section on PubMed or resources related to search term research.

Enjoy your literature review with these customized search results!
